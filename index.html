Newly Opened Dutch Bros Coffee in Lexington Faces Closure After Failing Health Inspection

South Carolina DHEC investigators cited multiple critical violations at the Lexington location, which opened just weeks ago, ordering the coffee chain to suspend operations immediately.

MR
Madison Rourke
WLXN Local Investigative Reporter
Published: Apr. 1, 2026, 9:48 a.m. ET
Updated: 12:47 p.m. ET
A paper notice posted on the front door of the Lexington Dutch Bros location Wednesday morning informed customers the store is temporarily closed pending re-inspection. (WLXN Staff)

LEXINGTON, S.C. — The Dutch Bros Coffee location in Lexington has been ordered to immediately cease food and beverage service after state health inspectors discovered several critical violations during a routine compliance inspection Tuesday afternoon, according to documents obtained by WLXN News 4.

The South Carolina Department of Health and Environmental Control (DHEC) conducted the unannounced inspection at approximately 2:30 p.m. on Tuesday and issued a closure order before the store's close of business. A notice was posted on the storefront door Wednesday morning, confirming customers who arrived for their morning coffee that the location remains shuttered.

The inspection report, a copy of which was provided to WLXN, lists a total of seven violations, four of which were classified as "Priority" — the most serious category under state health code — and three classified as "Priority Foundation" violations.

📋 Key Violations Cited by DHEC (April 1, 2025)

  • Temperature Control Failure: Multiple dairy and perishable products stored at unsafe temperatures, including milk and cold brew bases
  • Pest Activity: Evidence of rodent activity discovered in rear storage and dry goods area
  • Handwashing Non-Compliance: Handwashing station observed blocked and inaccessible to staff during service hours
  • Equipment Sanitization: Espresso and blending equipment found not properly sanitized between uses
  • Employee Hygiene: Staff observed handling ready-to-serve beverages without proper glove use or handwashing

"Any Priority violation requires immediate corrective action, and in cases where we observe multiple Priority violations during a single inspection, our protocol is to issue an emergency suspension of operations," said DHEC spokesperson Carla Jennings in a statement to WLXN. "The safety of the public is our top priority, and we take these situations very seriously regardless of the size or popularity of the establishment."

"We observed conditions that, frankly, were not what you'd expect from a brand-new facility. These things can happen during a rushed opening."

— Anonymous DHEC inspector, speaking off the record to WLXN News 4

The Lexington location, which opened to considerable fanfare just weeks ago, drew long lines of customers eager to try the Oregon-based chain's signature cold brew and energy drinks. Dutch Bros has been rapidly expanding across the Southeast, with the Lexington store representing one of several new South Carolina openings in recent months.

Dutch Bros corporate communications did not respond to WLXN's request for comment by time of publication. A voicemail left with the store's local manager had also not been returned as of Wednesday morning.

Several customers who arrived at the Lexington location Wednesday morning expressed frustration and surprise at the closure notice. "I drove 20 minutes for my Wednesday Rebel," said one woman, who declined to give her name. "I can't believe it. It's brand new — how do you already have this kind of problem?"

Under DHEC rules, the location must undergo a full re-inspection and receive a passing score before it can resume serving customers. There is no set timeline, though DHEC confirmed that operators typically schedule re-inspections within five to ten business days of a closure order. The agency added that a reinspection fee of $150 applies.
